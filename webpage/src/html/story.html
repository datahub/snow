How this Wisconsin winter compares to past seasons' snowfalls

By Andrew Mollica and Meg Jones

Anyone who has lived through winter in Wisconsin knows the snow spigot varies from a gush to a trickle. Sometimes snow piles up and doesn’t finally disappear until, like, June. And some years it seems that snow blowers and shovels hardly get a workout.

For Wisconsinites, especially natives or those who have spent many years here, storms sometimes blend and blur as memories get hazy. Was the pow-brrr-pow-brrr-pow of alternating snow storms and subzero temperatures in January 1979 worse than 1982’s early-January blizzard that dumped 16 inches within a few hours? Does everyone who survived the 1947 blizzard that basically paralyzed Milwaukee for weeks — with 18 inches of snow, 60 mph winds and 10-foot drifts — have bragging rights over the folks who lived through the blizzard of February 2008?

Below, take a tour of notable Milwaukee winters and explore the snowiest — and least-snowy — years since 1940.
